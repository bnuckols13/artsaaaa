Grounding Meditation (5 minutes)

Find a comfortable position. You might sit, stand, or lie down—whatever feels right for your body today.

Take three deep breaths...
In through your nose... Out through your mouth...
Let each exhale release any tension you're holding.

Notice your body's contact with what supports you.
Feel the ground beneath your feet, the chair beneath you, the weight of your body being held.

Bring awareness to your inner landscape...
Imagine your inner world as a vast, rich terrain. There are many parts of you that live here—some you know well, others you've barely met. Each one has wisdom. Each one has been trying to help you, in its own way.

Today, we'll meet some of these parts through different creative languages: through art, through movement, through sound, through words, through voice, and through open exploration.

Take one more deep breath... and when you're ready, let's begin.

Understanding Your Inner Family

Internal Family Systems (IFS) teaches us that we're not just one unified self, but a rich community of parts. Each part has developed to help us survive, cope, or protect something precious within us.

ᛗ

Managers

These parts run the show day-to-day. They plan, control, perfect, please others, and keep you safe through staying in charge. They work hard to prevent painful feelings from emerging.

Examples: The Inner Critic, The Perfectionist, The People-Pleaser, The Planner, The Caretaker

ᚠ

Firefighters

When painful feelings do break through, these parts jump into emergency action. They use distraction, numbing, or intensity to put out the fire of emotional pain—fast.

Examples: The Escapist, The Rebel, The Numb-er, The Binge-r, The Rage, The Dissociator

ᛖ

Exiles

These are the young, vulnerable parts that carry painful emotions and memories. They hold our shame, fear, sadness, and loneliness. The other parts work to keep them locked away—but they long to be seen.

Examples: The Wounded Child, The Abandoned One, The Ashamed Part, The Lonely Part, The Scared Part

ᛋ

The Self

Beneath all the parts is your core Self—the wise, compassionate, curious you. The Self can hold all parts with care, help them feel seen, and gently guide the inner family toward healing.

Qualities of Self: Curiosity, Compassion, Calm, Clarity, Confidence, Courage, Creativity, Connectedness

◆ Important: There are no "bad" parts. Every part developed to help you. Even the ones that cause pain today were once trying to protect something precious in you.

Visual Arts Studio

⧗ Suggested time: 20-30 minutes

△ Art Prompts for Parts Work

  • Parts Portrait: Draw or collage 3-5 different parts of you. Give each one a visual form, color, size, and placement on the page. What does each one look like? Where do they live in relation to each other?
  • Part's Landscape: If one part of you had its own world, what would it look like? Draw or paint this inner landscape.
  • Color Mapping: Assign a color to different parts. Create an abstract color composition showing how these parts interact, overlap, or stay separate.
  • Before & After: Draw how your parts felt before you understood them vs. now that you're getting curious about them.
  • The Part I'm Meeting Today: Focus on one part. Give it visual form. What are its features? Its textures? Its energy?
◇ Materials you might use: Paper, markers, colored pencils, crayons, magazine images, scissors, glue, paint, pastels—whatever you have available. Digital drawing works too!
◈ Remember: If drawing feels like too much today, you can simply close your eyes and imagine the colors and shapes of your parts, then write about what you "saw" in your mind's eye.

◬
Movement Space

⧗ Suggested time: 15-25 minutes

◬ Movement Prompts for Parts Work

  • Embodying Parts: Move as 3 different parts of you. How does each one move? What's their tempo, their weight, their flow? Notice the transitions between them.
  • Laban Exploration: Choose a part. Explore it through different movement qualities: light vs. strong, direct vs. indirect, sudden vs. sustained, bound vs. free. Which qualities match this part?
  • Parts Conversation: Let two parts have a movement dialogue. They might approach, retreat, mirror, oppose, or dance together.
  • Frozen Sculptures: Create a still shape with your body that represents a part. Hold it. Feel what it's like to be this part. Then gently release and shake it out.
  • Journey Through Parts: Move continuously for 5-10 minutes, letting different parts emerge and shift. Notice who shows up.

Select Movement Qualities (click any that fit your parts):

Gentle Strong Quick Slow Flowing Bound Expansive Contracted Spiraling Shaking Grounded Floating
◇ Note: You can do this sitting, standing, or lying down. Movement doesn't have to be big—even micro-movements and gestures count. Let your body have its own intelligence.
◈ Not feeling movement today? You can imagine moving as your parts, or simply notice what impulses arise in your body as you think about them.

Sound Sanctuary

⧗ Suggested time: 20-30 minutes

≋ Sound & Music Prompts

  • Parts Playlist: Create a playlist where each song represents a different part. What's the Manager's song? The Exile's? The Firefighter's? The Self's?
  • Sonic Portrait: If a part of you made sound, what would it sound like? Use your voice, hum, sing, tap, or imagine instruments. Create a soundscape for 2-3 parts.
  • Rhythm Exploration: Give each part a rhythm. Clap, tap, or drum it out. What's the tempo of your Inner Critic? Your Playful Child? Your Wise Self?
  • Musical Dialogue: Have two parts "speak" through humming, toning, or singing. What do they sound like in conversation?
  • Song Association: Listen to 3-5 songs and notice which parts of you resonate with each one. What feelings, memories, or parts get activated?

Musical Qualities for Your Parts:

◇ High Energy / Fast Tempo Manager, Firefighter
◆ Soft / Gentle / Slow Exile, Vulnerable Part
◈ Rhythmic / Driving / Powerful Protector, Warrior Part
◉ Spacious / Ambient / Flowing Self, Wise Observer
◊ Intense / Chaotic / Loud Angry Part, Reactive Part
◇ Resources: Use Spotify, YouTube, Apple Music, or just your own voice. You can also use household objects as instruments or work in silence and imagine sounds.
◈ Sound-sensitive today? You can work in silence and write about what you imagine the sounds would be like, or focus on one very quiet sound.

Writing Chamber

⧗ Suggested time: 20-30 minutes

ᛋ Writing Prompts for Parts Work

  • Letter from a Part: Choose one part and let it write you a letter. What does it want you to know? What has it been trying to tell you?
  • Parts Dialogue: Write a conversation between two parts. Let them speak freely. What do they say to each other?
  • Part's Origin Story: Choose a protective part (Manager or Firefighter) and write its origin story. When did it first appear? What was it protecting you from?
  • Meeting an Exile: Write from the perspective of Self meeting a young, exiled part. Approach with curiosity and compassion. What does this meeting look like?
  • Parts Poetry: Write a poem that captures the landscape of your inner family. Use metaphor, image, and feeling.
  • Part's Day: Describe a typical day as narrated by one of your parts. How does it experience your life?
◇ Writing Approach: Don't edit yourself. Let the words flow. You can use stream-of-consciousness, structured paragraphs, poetry, lists, or any form that feels right. This is for you, not for anyone else.
◈ Writing feels stuck? Try writing with your non-dominant hand, or dictate into your phone's voice recorder and transcribe later. Or simply make a list of one-word descriptions for each part.

Drama Studio

⧗ Suggested time: 15-25 minutes

⚯ Drama & Voice Prompts

  • Voice Qualities: Speak as 3-4 different parts. What does each voice sound like? Is it loud or quiet? Fast or slow? High or low? What's the tone—critical, soothing, anxious, playful?
  • Parts on Stage: Imagine your parts as characters in a play. What would they say? What scenes would they enact? Speak their lines out loud or in a whisper.
  • Hot Seat: Sit in a chair and "become" one part at a time. Answer questions from the perspective of that part: "What do you want?" "What are you afraid of?" "What do you need?"
  • Role Reversal: Speak as your Inner Critic, then switch and speak back to it as your Self. Have a dialogue.
  • Parts Monologue: Give one part a 2-3 minute monologue. Let it tell its full story in first person.
  • Sound & Fury: Let an intense part (anger, fear, urgency) express itself through vocal toning, shouting into a pillow, or making non-word sounds.

Voice Exploration Chart

Part Voice Quality Key Phrase
Inner Critic Harsh, fast, judgmental "You're not good enough"
Anxious Part High-pitched, rapid, tight "What if something bad happens?"
Young Exile Soft, small, timid "Please don't leave me"
Wise Self Calm, grounded, spacious "I'm here with you"
◇ Note: This can feel silly or vulnerable! That's okay. You can do this alone in a private space, whisper, or even mouth the words silently. The point is to give parts a voice.
◈ Voice work feels too vulnerable? Write what the parts would say instead, or simply notice what voice tone arises when you think of each part—you don't have to actually speak.

Intuitive Exploration

⧗ Suggested time: Open-ended

✧ Following Your Creative Impulse

This station has no prescribed path. You're invited to explore your parts in whatever way emerges naturally. This might combine multiple modalities, or it might be something entirely unique to you.

Trust your instinct. What wants to happen? What medium is calling you? What part wants to be witnessed in its own way?

Some Possibilities (not prescriptions):

  • Create an altar or assemblage for a part using found objects
  • Dance while drawing, letting movement inform the visual art
  • Write poetry while listening to music that represents different parts
  • Create a multimedia collage combining image, text, and symbolic objects
  • Build a sandtray or 3D sculpture showing your inner landscape
  • Combine voice recording with visual journaling
  • Create a "parts shrine" with photos, objects, colors, and words
  • Make a video or digital story featuring your parts
  • Simply sit in contemplation and see what wants to emerge
◇ Remember: There is no wrong way to do this. This is about following what feels alive and true for you. Let your Self lead the way.
◈ Sometimes the most intuitive thing is to rest. If that's what your system needs, that's perfect. You can simply sit with the question: "What do my parts need right now?" and let the answer be stillness.
